SPACE-TIME PATCH-BASED HOLE FILLING WITH RANDOM WALKS IN VIEW SYNTHESIS

Sunghwan Choi, Bumsub Ham, and Kwanghoon Sohn
School of Electrical and Electronic Eng., Yonsei University, Seoul, Korea

In this paper, we propose an exemplar-based hole filling method in view synthesis in which a temporal consistency is enforced within a random walks framework. As opposed to the conventional inpainting where the filling priority is curtail, the sample space also influences the performance of inpainting in case of filling a disoccluded region in view synthesis. The proposed method first classifies boundary along the hole region whether it belongs to the foreground or background. By using this boundary classification as initial seeds, each individual frame of video sequence is then automatically labeled as a foreground or a background within a random walk framework. The background region is then only used as the sample space in the inpainting process. Furthermore, most existing methods consider a frame-wise consistency only, and neglects inter-frame relations, which results in flicker artifacts. In order to maintain a temporal consistency, the video is viewed of as a space-time volume, so that the space-time patch can be used in a unified framework. The experimental results show that the proposed method is superior to the state-of-the-art methods, and provides both spatially and temporally consistent results without any annoying flicker artifacts.
